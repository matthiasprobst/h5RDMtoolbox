Wrapper + ConventionsÂ¶

To meet the sustainable (FAIR) principles of data management, the package supports the usage of conventions. Conventions can be naming conventions or ontologies.

Such conventions work together with HDF5 wrapper classes - or the other way around: A wrapper class is associated with a convention. Generally, conventions are rules how to name HDF5 objects, which attributes are required for datasets and/or groups and optionally which values they can take leading to fully described data.

One such existing convention is the CF-conventions (cfconventions.org/) introduced and used by the Climate and Forecast (CF) community. Here, variables must be associated with the specific attributes, one of which (and the central one) is the standard_name. The h5rdmtoolbox has implemented the concept in a similar manner. As at this stage of deveopment no claim for completeness is given, the convention is called CF-like.
